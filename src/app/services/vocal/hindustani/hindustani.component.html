Hindustani

Hindustani classical music is the art music of northern regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is also called as North Indian classical music or “Śāstriya Saṅgīt”.

Hindustani music spots more prominence on spontaneity and exploring all aspects of a raga, while Carnatic music is primarily composition-based. The central notion in both these systems is that of a melodic raga, sung to a rhythmic cycle or tala. It is melodic music, with no concept of harmony. Indian classical music has seven basic notes with five interspersed half-notes, resulting in a 12-note scale. Unlike the 12-note scale in Western music, the base frequency of the scale is not fixed, and intertonal gaps may also vary. The performance is set to a melodic pattern called a raga characterized.
