Mridangam

The mridangam, also known as Tannumai, is a percussion instrument from India of ancient origin.

The mridangam is a double-sided drum whose body is usually made using a hollowed piece of jackfruit wood about an inch thick.

It is the primary rhythmic sound track in a Carnatic music cast. The two sided of the hollow jack fruit wood is covered a goatskin and laced to each other with leather straps around the circumference of the drum. These straps are put into a state of high tension to stretch out the circular membranes on either side of the hull, allowing them to resonate when struck.
